📋 Workshop Procedure

  1. Teacher chooses one student's prototype draft.
  2. The writer shares their question about their draft.
  3. The class workshops the draft — the writer stays quiet and takes notes.
  4. Everyone decides on changes to make to their own draft.

We'll workshop 2–3 drafts this way.

Constructive Feedback Sentence Starters

Compliments

  • I loved …
  • It was so cool the way you …
  • My favorite part was …

Suggestions

  • What would happen if …
  • Maybe you could make it even stronger by …
  • Maybe if you tried …
  • I was a little confused by …
  • It would be helpful if …

✍️ Then — Revise Your Draft

Follow the steps on the Unit 4 Revision Checklist. Use what you learned in the workshop. Aim for 10 minutes of focused writing.

Station 3

Battle Simulation

⏱ 10 minutes ⚔️ Odysseus vs. The Labyrinthian Hydra

A preview of next week's prototype testing. The teacher plays Odysseus. A student volunteer plays the monster. Everyone else votes.

📜 Monster Description — The Labyrinthian Hydra

Appearance. A massive creature as tall as a two-story house. Muscular body covered in tough, dark dragon-like scales. Twisted horns, fierce eyes, mouth dripping with poisonous spit. Six long necks, each ending in a snake-like head with reflective eyes.

Abilities. Cut off one head — two grow back. Venomous bite paralyzes. Can manipulate its surroundings to create a shifting maze.

Weaknesses. Can only be escaped from, never defeated. Vulnerable to distraction (regains focus quickly). Even fire — which it is immune to — frightens it because it doesn't know fire can't hurt it.

🎒 Odysseus's Inventory

Sword · Shield · Cloak · Armor

Leather pouch on belt:

1 rope 1 match 1 rock 1 lute 1 friendship bracelet 3 gold pieces 1 joke book

⚔️ Battle Procedure

  1. Places. Fighters start face to face in the monster's environment.
  2. 1st Moves. Odysseus moves first. Monster responds.
  3. 2nd Moves. Odysseus moves. Monster responds.
  4. Guide Revealed. Odysseus reads the How-to Guide.
  5. 3rd Moves. Final move. Monster responds. Battle ends.
  6. Vote. Spectators decide the winner.

Reminder: Moves describe actions, not results. "The monster swings its tail" ✅. "The tail knocks Odysseus unconscious" ❌. No one dies — Homer needs them alive!

How-to Guide: Outsmarting the Labyrinthian Hydra

  1. Understand Regeneration. Cutting off a head only makes things worse. Pin or trap heads instead of chopping.
  2. Befriend or Distract. Try kindness first. If that fails, use a distraction (a match works).
  3. Outwit the Hydra. Distract heads with riddles, puzzles, or stories.
  4. Remember Its Motivation. The Hydra wants to live. Make it clear you mean no harm.
  5. Navigate the Maze. Watch the eyes of each head — reflections show the correct path.
  6. Befriend Other Creatures. The Monstra Catastropha's itchy footprints can distract the Hydra.
  7. Retreat Safely. Leave quickly. Don't harm it, or it may chase you.

Station 6

Reading Discussion

⏱ 10 minutes 📖 Tales from the Odyssey · Ch. 5–7

🌊 Why does Odysseus choose to sail close to Scylla?

Read the start of Chapter 5 (page 219, ending "...heads out of her cave").

Odysseus knows he can't fight Charybdis. Sailing closer to Scylla's cliff means losing fewer lives. He armors up hoping to protect his men — his hope is short-lived, but his strategic decision saved the majority.

☀️ What challenges do the men face on the Island of the Sun God?

Read p. 226 ("Odysseus, you are too strong!") to p. 227 ("...filled with dread"). Then p. 232 ("The Greek warriors..." to "...to set sail").

Exhaustion and hunger force them to land. They take oaths not to touch the sun god's animals. A storm pins them to the island longer than planned — they fight growing hunger and desperation, and their oaths are tested.

Station 7

Vocabulary

⏱ 10 minutes 📖 Workbook pages 20–21

Tap a card to flip it. Talk about each word, give examples, then have students restate in their own words and sketch a symbol.

cower
[KOW-ur]
verb
to crouch down or shrink back in fear, typically in a frightened or submissive way.
"The villagers cowered as the Hydra's shadow passed overhead."
pithicus
[PITH-ih-kis]
word root
of or related to apes or monkeys.
australopithecus = "southern ape"
raptor
[RAP-ter]
word root
of or relating to an animal that "steals" as a predator or hunter.
velociraptor — the swift hunter dinosaur
rex
[recks]
word root
of or relating to an animal that is impressive — like a king or ruler.
tyrannosaurus rex — the king of the tyrant lizards
odon
[oh-don]
word root
of or relating to many or large teeth.
megalodon — the giant-toothed shark
⚡ Bonus Quest
Trading cards
optional
Combine roots into your own monster. Give it a "Cower Score" rating!
e.g., Pithico-rex-odon — a kingly ape with massive teeth.
